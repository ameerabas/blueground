About This Playbook

This playbook is the operating companion to the Blueground (M) Sdn Bhd — Business Roadmap & Capital Plan. Where the roadmap makes the case to shareholders and Wthr for the capital required and the path to breakeven, this document translates that plan into a month-by-month operating manual for the General Manager: how many apartments should be under management, how the team should be staffed, what the wage bill and other operating costs should look like, what revenue and margin the month should produce, and what the bank balance should be at the start and end of the month.

If the General Manager executes each month according to this playbook, the business should track the Business Roadmap's trajectory to operational breakeven at Month 41 (February 2029) and a stabilized, cash-generative business by Month 46 (July 2029).

How To Use This Playbook

  • One section per month. Each month from August 2026 (Month 11) through June 2029 (Month 45) has its own page(s), headed by the calendar month and its Month Number (consistent with the numbering used in the Business Roadmap, where Month 1 = October 2025).
  • Start every month by checking the KPI strip and the action banners at the top of that month's section. Red banners marked ACTION REQUIRED mean a capital tranche must be formally requested from Wthr that month — do this early in the month, not at month-end, since the cash is needed to fund that month's operating costs and new-unit setup. Green banners flag staffing actions (new hires) required that month.
  • Use the tables as your monthly budget. The Staffing and Other Operating Costs tables are the wage bill and non-staff opex the business should be running at that month; the Revenue Forecast and Net Margin are the targets to manage the team toward — not guarantees, but the numbers the capital plan and tranche sizing assume.
  • Reconcile the bank balance every month-end. The Cash Reconciliation block shows the expected beginning balance, all inflows and outflows, and the expected ending balance. If actual cash is materially behind this figure, escalate to the Board before the following month's tranche request — the tranche schedule was sized against this exact trajectory and has limited slack.
  • Treat the target margin as a pricing and mix discipline, not a hope. Margin is not assumed to improve on its own — it requires deliberate pricing decisions and a growing share of higher-margin corporate bookings, described in the Standing Instructions below.

Standing Instructions (Apply Every Month Unless Noted Otherwise)

  • Onboard approximately 6 net new apartments this month, maintaining a mix of roughly 80% Fixed Lease / 20% Revenue Share (i.e. 2 of every 10 new units on a revenue-share basis).
  • Assume new Fixed Lease units are secured 50% furnished / 50% unfurnished; budget accordingly (furnished upfront cost ≈RM 18,750, unfurnished ≈RM 27,500).
  • Hold pricing and corporate-mix discipline to track that month's target net margin — the roadmap assumes margin improves linearly from −75.1% to +15% between Month 11 and Month 46; falling behind this curve increases the capital the business will need to request.
  • Ensure statutory payroll contributions (KWSP/EPF, SOCSO, SIP) of 15% are filed correctly on the full payroll every month.
  • Watch the portfolio size against the staffing thresholds: +1 Cleaning Crew member for every 20 apartments added beyond the current base, and the Maintenance Person, Assistant CS Associate, Assistant Inbound Sales Associate, and Accountant all join once the portfolio reaches 30 units.
  • Do not draw down cash reserves below the level shown in that month's Cash Reconciliation without Board notice — the tranche schedule keeps a deliberately thin buffer at points in the timeline (as low as ≈RM 102,000 ahead of Month 17) and has no slack for unplanned shortfalls.
